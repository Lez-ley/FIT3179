The Changing Face of

Malaysia's Household Income

Located at the crossroads of Southeast Asia, Malaysia is a nation defined by its diversity in culture, geography, and economy. Since independence, the country has shifted from an agriculture-based economy toward industrialization and services, lifting millions into the middle class. Yet, as Malaysia's economy has grown, income distribution and regional disparities have emerged as defining challenges. Different states have developed at varying paces, and while overall prosperity has improved, questions remain about how evenly that growth has been shared.

Malaysia Is Rich, But Is The Growth Fair ?

a look at Malaysia's rising incomes and the unequal pace of growth across households

Mean and Median Household Income, 1974 - 2022

The chart depicts Malaysia's mean and median household income growth from 1974 to 2022.

Both indicators show a long-term upward trend, with noticeable declines during the 1997 Asian Financial Crisis and the 2020 Covid-19 pandemic. THe persistent disparity between mean and median income highlights enduring income inequality within the country.

How Are Incomes Growing and Slowing, 1974 - 2022

The chart shows the fluctuations in Malaysia's household income growth rate from 1974 to 2022.

Periods of rapid expansion, such as the early 1980s, are contrasted by sharp declines during economic crises, notably in 1987, 1999, and 2020. The irregular pattern indicates that Malaysa's income growth is highly sensitive to domestic and global economic conditions.
